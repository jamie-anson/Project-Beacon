Project Beacon

Trust AI with evidence, not assumptions.

What

An independent, open platform where anyone can run tests to see if an AI gives different answers depending on where you are.

It’s open to any benchmark or question — we don’t decide what gets tested, and we don’t control the results.

Why

  • Trust needs proof. Incentives, politics, and bad actors can distort reality. People deserve verifiable evidence.
  • Power to the user. Everyone should be able to validate, diagnose, and question AI systems.
  • Truth over narratives. Independent, repeatable tests show what models actually do.

The problem we’re solving

  • Opacity: It’s hard to tell if a model is good, safe, or fair—and for whom.
  • Inconsistency: Answers vary across locations, throttled networks, and policy settings.
  • Control points: Vendors, states, and intermediaries can influence availability, speed, and output.

Who’s impacted

  • People: Depend on AI for decisions, work, learning; deserve transparent behavior.
  • Automation systems: Need consistent outputs; instability causes failures and cost.
  • Robots: Physical systems require reliable perception and planning.

How

We combine decentralised compute, cryptographic proofs, and permanent storage to make results trustworthy and tamper-evident:

  • Verifiable job specs

    Clear, reproducible test definitions.

  • Signed receipts

    Cryptographic proof each job ran exactly as specified.

  • Public diff reports

    Side-by-side comparison of outputs by region.

  • Independent human attesters

    Validating that results match the claims.

  • Permanent storage

    Results preserved on decentralised networks (e.g. IPFS, blockchain) for public review and audit.

  • Distributed infrastructure

    All tests run on the decentralised Golem Network to avoid single points of control.

Open methods • Real-world conditions • User-verifiable

What we benchmark and test

  • AI capability benchmarks: Standardized, task-based suites (e.g., SWE-bench-like) for reasoning, coding, and problem-solving.
  • Network integrity tests: Latency, throughput, throttling detection, regional routing, and availability over time.
  • Diagnostic probes: Model versioning, rate limits, policy toggles, and guardrail behavior.
  • Answer consistency: Do users in different geographies get the same answers to the same questions?

Threat model

  • AI companies: Are we getting the model we were sold? Which model is best for my use case and region? Is performance consistent?
  • Nation states: Do geopolitics, sanctions, or wars change access, speed, or responses?
  • Well-meaning politicians: Do policy presets constrain useful answers or inject ideological bias?
  • Man-in-the-middle attackers: VPN/ISP throttling, logging, service spoofing, and DNS poisoning.

Our principles

  • Independence: No vendor pay-to-win. Methods are public; results are auditable.
  • Reproducibility: Same test, same setup, same outcome—documented and verifiable.
  • Neutrality: We measure; you decide. Clear metrics, no spin.
  • Safety and inclusion: Benchmarks consider regional access, policy filters, and user needs.
  • Permanence: Results, methods, and key metadata are cryptographically signed and anchored to a public ledger for tamper-evident provenance and long-term availability.

MVP

Our first benchmark — “Who are you?” — will test a simple identity question across locations to prove the end-to-end flow works. From there, anyone can submit their own.

Why collaborate

We’re looking for collaborators who share our commitment to truth, transparency, and trust in AI. Whether you’re into distributed computing, AI safety, or independent auditing — this project needs your perspective.

Our long-term vision is to establish the platform as a globally trusted standard for AI output verification, recognised by international bodies such as the United Nations.
